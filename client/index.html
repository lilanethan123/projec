Germany: 1933 - 1945

A brief overview of a tumultuous era that changed the world forever.

1933

The Rise to Power

In January 1933, a pivotal shift occurred in German history. The Weimar Republic, weakened by economic instability and political turmoil, was dismantled. A new totalitarian regime emerged, centralizing power and suspending civil liberties.

This period saw rapid industrialization and militarization, defying international treaties of the time. The state apparatus took control of all aspects of society, from the press to education, mobilizing the nation for a vision of expansion and dominance.

1939

Global Conflict

On September 1, 1939, forces invaded Poland, marking the beginning of World War II. Utilizing "Blitzkrieg" (lightning war) tactics, the military campaign moved with unprecedented speed and coordination, overwhelming defenses across Europe.

For six long years, the world was engulfed in total war. Fronts opened in the West, East, North Africa, and beyond. It was a conflict of industrial scale, reshaping national borders and human history.

1945

The Downfall

By 1945, the tide had turned irrevocably. Allied forces pressed from the West and Soviet forces from the East. The infrastructure of the regime crumbled under the weight of a multi-front war.

Berlin fell in May 1945, bringing an end to the conflict in Europe. The aftermath left a continent in ruins and divided, leading to the formation of the United Nations and the beginning of the Cold War era.
